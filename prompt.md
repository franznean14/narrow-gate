This is a significant and exciting pivot! You are moving from a Linear Race Game (like Candy Land or The Game of Life) to a Tactical Cooperative Survival Game (like Pandemic, Forbidden Island, or Spirit Island).

This new direction aligns much better with your goal of teaching interdependency and unity. In a race game, players inherently want to move forward. In a node-based survival game, players must move toward each other to survive.

Here is a breakdown of how this new mechanic can work, suggestions for the turn structure, and how to handle the "Closeness Factor."

1. The Core Loop: "Stand Firm & Gather Together"
This structure balances the randomness of trials with the strategy of unity.

The Decks (Revised)
To keep the table clean, we consolidate into two main draw sources:

The Adversary Deck (Red/Black Back): Contains Challenges (the main threat), Circumstances (modifiers), and Special Events (Great Tribulation).

The Supply Deck (Blue/Gold Back): Contains Prayer (wildcards), Qualities/Character (permanent buffs), Faith in Action (trivia/actions), and Personal Trials (immediate negative attachments).

2. The Mechanics Breakdown
The "Closeness" Map (The Board)
Instead of a straight line, imagine a Network Graph (like a spiderweb or a constellation).

Nodes: There are ~10-12 nodes representing spiritual locations or abstract states (e.g., "The Congregation," "The Ministry," "Secular Work," "Home," "Trial").

Connections: Lines connect these nodes. Some are close (1 step), some far (3 steps).

Isolating Factors: Some Adversary cards might break a connection line, forcing players to take the long way around to help a friend.

The "Faith in Action" (Trivia Mechanic)
The Risk: Trivia in games can sometimes make people feel bad if they don't know the answer, or it stalls the game.

The Solution: Make the Trivia a Bonus Multiplier.

Example: You play a "Preaching" card to help the group. It contributes 1 Point to the goal automatically.

The Action: You can choose to "Answer a Question."

Roll Dice: (1-3 = Easy, 4-6 = Hard).

Result: If you answer correctly, the card counts for 3 Points instead of 1. If you fail, it stays at 1 point.

Why this works: It rewards knowledge without punishing those who are learning.

Immediate vs. Held Cards
To answer your question:

Positive Cards (Prayer, Qualities, Action): Go into the player's Hand. This allows for strategy ("I'll save this strong card for the Great Tribulation").

Negative Cards (Personal Trials): If drawn from the Supply pile, they are Played Immediately in front of the player.

Effect: "Burden." While this card is in front of you, you cannot move or you draw 1 less card.

Resolution: Another player within 2 nodes must discard a card to remove it from you. (Enforces closeness).

3. Proposed Turn Structure
Here is a 4-step flow that creates the "2 cycle" pacing you mentioned.

Phase 1: The Scene is Set (Start of Round)

Reveal Challenge: Draw top card of Adversary Deck (e.g., "Economic Hardship"). It requires 10 Supply Points to overcome.

Reveal Circumstance: Draw next card (e.g., "Bad Weather" - Movement costs double this round).

Phase 2: Player Actions (The Turn) Each player takes a turn (going clockwise). On their turn, they have 3 Action Points (AP) to spend:

Move (1 AP): Move pin 1 node.

Encourage (1 AP): Give a card from hand to a player on the same or adjacent node.

Use Supply (1 AP):

Play a Quality: Place on table (Permanent Buff).

Contribute to Challenge: Discard a card to add points to the round goal.

Faith in Action: Play card + Roll Dice + Answer Question for massive points.

Draw (End of Turn): Draw back up to hand limit (e.g., 5 cards). If a Personal Trial is drawn, reveal immediately.

Phase 3: The Adversary Strikes (End of Round) If the Challenge is not overcome by the end of the round:

Consequence: The Challenge triggers a negative effect (e.g., "Lose 1 Unity" or "Everyone discards 1 card").

Progress: The Challenge stays active for the next round (accumulating difficulty).

Phase 4: Unity Check

If the Challenge was overcome: Count how many players are within 2 nodes of each other.

Reward: Gain Unity Points based on that number.

4. Special Rounds (The Climax)
You mentioned Great Tribulation and Armageddon. These shouldn't just be "harder," they should change the rules.

Great Tribulation (Mid-Game Boss):

Trigger: Shuffled into the bottom half of the Adversary Deck.

Armageddon (Final Boss):

Objective: Survival. The goal is no longer to "solve" the challenge, but to Hold Fast.

Mechanic: Players must endure 3 rounds of intense attacks without Unity dropping to zero. Prayer cards drawn during this phase count double.
